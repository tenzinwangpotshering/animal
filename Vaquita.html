Vaquite

What is a vaquite?

-The vaquita is a species of porpoise endemic to the northern end of the Gulf of California in Baja California, Mexico. Reaching a maximum body length of 150 cm or 140 cm, it is the smallest of all living cetaceans.

Fun facts about vaquite.

1.Only 10 Vaquitas Remain in the World.

2.The Vaquita Is the Smallest Cetacean Species In the World.

3.Vaquitas Are Uniquely Adapted to Live in Warm Waters.

4.The Vaquita Is Found In Only One Place In the World.

5.Vaquitas Are Extremely Important to the Marine Ecosystem.

Where the vaquite is located?

The vaquita has the smallest geographical range of any marine mammal. It only lives in the northern part of the Gulf of California in Mexico. Most vaquitas live east of the town of San Felipe, Baja California, within a 1,519-square-mile area that is less than one-fourth the size of metropolitan Los Angeles.
