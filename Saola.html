Saola

What is a saola?

The saola, also called spindlehorn, Asian unicorn, or infrequently, Vu Quang bovid, is one of the world's rarest large mammals, a forest-dwelling bovine native to the Annamite Range in Vietnam and Laos.

Fun facts of saola.

Saola are the first large mammal new to science in over 50 years.

Their horns are twice the length of their head.

It's estimated that there are no more than 750 remaining.

There are none in captivity.

The saola (Pseudoryx nghetinhensis), also called spindlehorn.

Where are saola located?

Saola have striking white markings on the face and large glands on the muzzle, which may be used to mark their territory or attract mates. They are found only in the Annamite Mountains of Laos and Viet Nam.
