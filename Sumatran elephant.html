Sumatran elephant

What is a Sumatran elephant?

The Sumatran elephant is one of three recognized subspecies of the Asian elephant, and native to the Indonesian island of Sumatra.

Fun facts about sumatran elephant.

They're the world's largest land animal.

You can tell the three species apart by their ears.

Their trunks have mad skills.

Their tusks are actually teeth.

They've got thick skin.

Where are sumatran elephants locaed?

Sumatran Elephant (Elephas maximus ssp. sumatranus) is the subspecies of Asian Elephant, one of two species of elephant in the world. They dwell in lowland forest of Sumatra in province of Riau, West Sumatra, and Lampung, most of it is outside a protected area.
